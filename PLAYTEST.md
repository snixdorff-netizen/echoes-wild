# ECHOES Wildlife Acoustics – 20-Person Playtest Kit

**Version**: Playtest build (June 2026)  
**Game file**: `index.html` (single self-contained HTML)  
**Goal of test**: Validate fun, education value, usability, viral potential, and retention hooks with real users before further development or Unity port.

## Overview & Timeline
- Total participants: 20 (balanced mix)
- Session length: 12–20 minutes
- Format: Remote (screen share + mic) or in-person
- Tools: Browser + the index.html (double-click or local server recommended)
- Data: In-game "Feedback" button (copies JSON + prompts) + observer notes + post-survey

**Recruitment targets**:
- 5 naturalists / birders / wildlife enthusiasts
- 5 students or educators (K-12 / college)
- 5 gamers (casual or mid-core)
- 5 general public (no strong prior interest)

**Incentives**: None required, but thank-you note or small donation to wildlife org is nice.

## Session Script (moderator / self-guided)

### Welcome (1 min)
"Thanks for helping test ECHOES. This is a work-in-progress single-file game about wildlife acoustics. Your honest feedback will shape it.

Please think aloud as much as you can — say what you're trying, what surprises you, what frustrates you.

No wrong answers. Ready to start?"

### Warm-up (2 min)
- Open the game.
- "Just explore the Forest habitat for a minute or two. Move around, listen, see what happens."
- Note: first audio click required.

### Core Tasks (8-12 min)
Encourage think-aloud.

1. **Learn the basics**  
   "Try to log at least 3 species in the Forest during daytime. Use the listen cone (hold L or mouse). What do you notice about sound?"

2. **Time & technique**  
   "Advance time to night (T key or button). Try to record a bat or owl. Get close and face the animal while holding listen. How does the cone feel?"

3. **Full completion + switch**  
   "Now switch to a different habitat (Marsh or Canyon). See if you can fully complete it — log all 6 species. Take your time."

4. **Share moment**  
   "When you finish a habitat (or get close), try the 'Copy Field Report' or 'Report' button. What do you think of sharing something like that?"

### Free play (optional 2-3 min)
"Anything else you'd like to try? Different times, more recordings?"

### Wrap (2 min)
"Any last thoughts before we do the short survey?"

**End recording / notes.**

## Observation Guide (moderator notes)
Track:
- Moments of delight (laugh, "cool!", leaning in)
- Confusion / friction points (hesitation, wrong clicks, "what do I do?")
- Audio reactions ( "that actually sounded like a bat")
- Icon / visual feedback ("they're big and cute")
- Cone / technique understanding (do they get "face + close"?)
- Completion success and time
- Use of share / feedback
- Any crashes, audio fail, mobile issues

Rate overall:
- Fun (1-10)
- Clarity of goal
- Audio quality / satisfaction
- Desire to continue or share

## Post-Play Survey (copy into Google Form or send as text)

**Basic**
1. Age range / occupation (optional)
2. Prior interest in birds / nature / games? (1-5)

**Experience**
3. How fun was the game overall? (1-10)
4. How clear were the goals and controls? (1-10)
5. Did you feel like you were "learning" something about listening to animals? (yes / somewhat / no) + one sentence

**Specifics**
6. What was the most satisfying moment? (e.g. first correct ID, bat call, completing a habitat)
7. What was most frustrating or confusing?
8. How did the directional listen (green cone) feel? Useful? Fun?
9. Would you share a "Field Report" with friends or post online? Why or why not?

**Open**
10. Any other comments, ideas, or things that felt missing?

**Optional for remote**: screen recording consent.

## Data Collection Workflow
1. Participant uses in-game **Feedback** button at end of session → pastes into notes / form.
2. You take observation notes during.
3. Participant fills survey.
4. Optional: quick 2-3 min voice note or chat.

Aggregate:
- Completion rates per task
- Average integrity on first full habitat
- % who would share
- Common delights / pain points
- Educational self-report

## Analysis & Next Steps
Look for:
- Is the core loop (listen → record → ID) satisfying within 15 min?
- Do big icons + cone deliver the "aha" moments?
- Does the share report create viral desire?
- Retention signals (streak, desire to try other habitats)
- Education transfer (can they explain why facing/close matters?)
- Technical: audio works across devices/browsers? Mobile playable?

Prioritize fixes for:
- Onboarding friction
- Audio clarity
- "I did it!" payoff
- Share virality

## Running the Test (logistics)
- Send file + short pre-instructions 1 day before.
- Run 3-5 per day max to avoid moderator fatigue.
- Use same device/browser notes if remote.
- Total calendar time: ~1 week for 20 if 4-5/day.

**Hosting tip**: For consistency, you can host the folder on a simple static site (Netlify drop, GitHub Pages, or just tell them to use the local server command).
